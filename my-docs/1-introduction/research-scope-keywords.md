## Research Scope

### Core Research Areas

**Physical AI**
- Embodied intelligence and its theoretical foundations
- Coupling between perception, action, and environment
- Sensorimotor learning in physical systems
- Physical cognition and reasoning

**Humanoid Robotics**
- Human-like morphology and its implications
- Biomechanics and human-inspired control
- Social interaction capabilities
- Human-robot interaction principles

**Embodied AI**
- Machine learning with physical embodiment
- Sensorimotor learning and development
- Morphological computation
- Environmental interaction models

### Technical Layer Research

**Mechanical Layer**
- Actuator technologies and their control
- Joint design and kinematics
- Transmission systems and efficiency
- Structural materials and lightweight design

**Electrical Layer**
- Power systems and energy management
- Sensor integration and signal processing
- Communication protocols and networks
- Safety and redundancy systems

**Control Layer**
- Low-level motor control
- High-level motion planning
- Balance and stability control
- Adaptive control techniques

**AI Layer**
- Perception systems (vision, audition, tactile)
- Cognitive architectures
- Learning algorithms for physical interaction
- Planning and decision-making in uncertain environments

## Research Boundaries

### In Scope
- Theoretical foundations of Physical AI
- Practical implementation of humanoid control systems
- Integration of AI and robotics
- Simulation and real-world validation
- Educational aspects of embodied systems

### Out of Scope
- General purpose AI without embodiment
- Industrial automation systems (non-humanoid)
- Pure software AI systems
- Non-robotic machine learning applications
- Detailed mechanical engineering outside robotics context

## Research Questions

### Foundational Questions
1. How does physical embodiment change learning and intelligence?
2. What are the computational advantages of embodied systems?
3. How can morphological computation be leveraged in humanoid design?

### Technical Questions
1. What are the most effective control architectures for humanoid robots?
2. How can perception and action be optimally integrated?
3. What simulation-to-reality transfer methods are most effective?

### Applied Questions
1. How can humanoid robots be made more socially acceptable?
2. What learning methods work best for physical interaction?
3. How can human-robot collaboration be optimized?

## Target Audience Considerations

### Academic Level
- Graduate students in robotics and AI
- Researchers transitioning from software AI to embodied systems
- Practitioners seeking to understand Physical AI

### Background Assumptions
- Basic understanding of machine learning concepts
- Familiarity with programming (especially Python)
- Interest in robotics applications
- Some exposure to mathematical concepts (linear algebra, calculus)
