## Chapter 1: Physical AI & Embodied Intelligence Sources

### Primary Sources for Chapter 1

1. **Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). "How the Body Shapes the Way We Think." MIT Press.**
   - Chapter 1: "Introduction: The Unfinished Cognitive Revolution"
   - Chapter 2: "The New Philosophy of Machine Intelligence"
   - Chapter 3: "Morphological Computation: Exploiting the Dynamics of Matter"
   - Focus: Foundational concepts of embodied cognition

2. **Brooks, R. A. (1991). "Intelligence without representation." Artificial Intelligence, 47(1-3), 139-159.**
   - Focus: Challenging symbolic AI approaches
   - Relevance: Foundation of embodied intelligence philosophy

3. **Clark, A. (2008). "Supersizing the Mind: Embodiment, Action, and Cognitive Extension." Oxford University Press.**
   - Chapter 2: "The Bounds of Cognition"
   - Chapter 3: "Knowing, Representing, and Doing"
   - Focus: Extended mind thesis and embodied cognition

4. **Hutto, D. D., & Myin, E. (2013). "Radicalizing Enactivism: Basic Minds without Content." MIT Press.**
   - Chapter 1: "Setting the Stage"
   - Chapter 4: "Making Sense of Basic Minds"
   - Focus: More embodied approaches to cognition

5. **Pfeifer, R., Lungarella, M., & Iida, F. (2007). "Self-organization, embodiment, and biologically inspired robotics." Science, 318(5853), 1088-1093.**

6. **Doncieux, S., Boucheny, D., & Meyer, J. A. (2010). "Continual and developmental learning in embodied agents." Frontiers in neurorobotics, 4, 20.**

8. **Coradeschi, S., Loutfi, A., & Wrede, B. (2013). "A short introduction to cognitive robotics." Foundations and Trends in Robotics, 2(3), 203-261.**

   - Application: Bridge between cognitive science and robotics

9. **Ziemke, T., & Sharkey, N. E. (2001). "A stroll through the worlds of robots and humans." Cybernetics and Systems, 32(2-3), 71-118.**

   - Application: Justification for embodied approaches
